THE FUTURE: CLEANER ENERGY FOR MANKIND

There has been a lot of debate, but the science is crystal clear: Emissions need to be reduced significantly to avoid the worst impacts of climate change. To be able to achieve this, the world must end our dependence on fossil fuels and start to use alternative sources of energy that are cleaner, accessible, affordable, reliable, and sustainable. Green and renewable energy sources are sustainable energy options to replace fossil fuels. Though green energy and renewable energy are used synonymously, there is a basic difference between them. Renewable energy is energy generated from infinite sources; Green energy is energy generated from infinite sources that does not produce carbon emissions or negatively impact the environment.

While most green energy sources are renewable, not all renewable energy sources are considered entirely green. For example, the industrialisation and deforestation involved in the process of building large hydro dams (>30MW) used to generate energy from fast flowing water makes hydropower a renewable but not green resource. Another example of energy source that is renewable but not green is biomass. It is not green because the combustion of biomass materials (wood, agricultural crops, animal manure, and human sewage) releases carbon dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and sulphur oxides into the atmosphere.

Nuclear energy is an energy source that is neither green nor renewable. A large amount of energy is released in nuclear fission when electromagnetic radiation is used to split the nucleus of a uranium atom (U-235). Though the process does not produce greenhouse gas emissions, it requires mining, extraction, and long-term storage of radioactive waste which are threats to the environment.

The perfect clean energy mix occurs where green energy meets renewable energy, such as with solar energy and wind energy.

HOW DOES IT WORK?

Clean energy is produced using naturally replenished and virtually inexhaustible sources like the sun and wind. It works by producing power without negative environmental impacts, such as the release of greenhouse gases like CO2 which causes climate change. Fully developed, clean energy drives advances to preserve and protect the environment for future generations. A lot of clean energy is also renewable, including wind power, solar powered energy generation, and small hydro resources.

WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

Our world runs on the energy we produce. Production of clean energy helps to reduce the impact of climate change because it allows us to generate the energy the world needs without the greenhouse gas emissions and negative environmental effects that come with fossil fuels. While clean, renewable sources preserve the world’s natural resources, they also reduce the risk of environmental disasters, such as fuel spills or natural gas leaks. It is possible to create reliable power supplies with fuel diversification, using different energy sources. This will enhance energy security, ensuring there is enough affordable and reliable energy to meet our demands.

WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS?

Clean energy provides many environmental and economic benefits as follows:

  • Environmental Benefits
    • Less global warming
    • Reduces air pollution
    • Uses renewable sources
  • Economic Benefits
    • Cost savings, as there is no need to extract and transport fuels
    • Creates jobs to develop, manufacture and install the clean energy resources of the future
    • Stable Energy Prices
  • Health Benefits
    • Reduces harmful air pollutants
    • Improves public health

WHAT ARE YOUR ROLES?

Everybody has a role to place in reducing the effects of climate change and create a cleaner energy future for the generation to come. We can work together to lower our carbon footprint by reducing the amount of greenhouse gases we put into the atmosphere. This means using less energy! We can:

  • Take the bus, ride a bike, or carpool.
  • Turn off lights and electronics when not using them.
  • Recycle and reuse products, since making new stuff requires more energy.
  • Plant a tree. Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the air.
  • Encourage communities and governments to switch to "clean" energy sources like solar , wind , nuclear , and geothermal power.

Some more activities to reduce your carbon footprint are captured in the Carbon Footprint section.

BE A CLIMATE AMBASSADOR

Climate change effects are being felt at all levels – local, national, and global. Becoming a Climate Ambassador will give the opportunity and platform to do something positive within your community and network.

Greta Thunberg is probably the most famous Climate Activist/Ambassador. She is known around the world for protesting the Swedish government by sitting outside the parliament every day during school hours in August 2018 demanding that carbon emissions be reduced in accordance with the Paris Agreement. She was 15 years old. Her action inspired a youth-led global climate strike movement. Another Climate Activist is Vanessa Nakate. She became a well-known face in a global movement of young people “striking” for the climate protesting on the streets of Uganda in 2019. She was appointed UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador in 2022.

As a Climate Ambassador, you will lead the way by:

  • Taking climate change discussions into your schools and local communities across Africa and by extension the world
  • Creating climate-focused clubs and groups to promote eco-education in schools
  • Mentoring younger students to become environmental stewards as they take on climate issues in their communities
  • Organising earth-friendly projects

For more resources and tools on how to be a Climate Ambassador, check out the Climate Kids Ambassadors website.

As we take on this life changing role to be a Climate Ambassador, we must be very intentional about our lifestyle choices and purchasing habits to reduce our carbon footprint. It is important, now more than ever, that we join hands together as children to educate others about climate change.

Popular Posts

  • What is Global Warming?
    Global warming is the term used to describe the rising of the average global temperature of the Earth that started in the pre-Industrial era due to the heating of the Earth’s surface. The Earth’s warming trend has been going on for a very long time, but the rate of warming has significantly increased in the last century mainly due to human activities. Human activities such as burning of fossil fuels to generate power and manufacture goods are causing worldwide temperatures to rise higher and faster than any time we know of in the past.
  • Causes OF Global Warming
    The main factor contributing to climate change is human activities. This is because of the large number of greenhouse gases we produce due to our lifestyle choices. Some greenhouse gases occur naturally in the atmosphere and play a part in making our planet habitable. However, the large number of greenhouse gases we produce is too much for the environment to handle.
  • Effects of Global Warming
    Warmer temperatures are gradually changing weather patterns and disrupting the normal balance of nature. Some of these changes such as heat waves, wildfires, prolonged drought, and extreme rainfall are happening faster than scientists previously predicted resulting in loss of lives, property damage and population displacement. Extreme weather and climate change pose many risks to human beings and all other forms of life on Earth.
  • Why should we care?
    Although climate change is affecting everyone, the concern is its effects on future generations especially children and young people in Africa who will most likely bear the brunt in the coming decades. Scientists estimate that the worst is yet to come and predict a warmer atmosphere and ocean if we do not reduce emissions. If global warming is not stopped or slowed down now, world economies could become stagnant as governments and investors divert funding from critical services such as health, education, nutrition, and other social services which would yield benefits for future generations to mitigate the climate crisis.
